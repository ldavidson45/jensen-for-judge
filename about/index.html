Meet Linda

Linda Collins Jensen is a lifelong Mobilian, a community leader, and a candidate for Mobile County's Juvenile Court Judge.

Linda has served as Mobile County's Juvenile Court Referee since 2010. Over the past decade in this role, she has dedicated her career to helping the youth and families of Mobile. In 2011, Linda founded the Mobile County Juvenile Court Girls Leadership Program which is designed to provide young women on probation with strong female mentorship to help them focus on positive future goals.

Linda has decades of experience in family and juvenile law. Early in her career, she served as a special child abuse prosecutor for the Mobile County District Attorney's office. Prior to her appointment as Juvenile Court Referee, Linda worked as a private practice lawyer focusing on criminal, juvenile, and family law.

Community Involvement And Organizations

  • Program Coordinator

    Girls Leadership Program

    A group mentoring program designed to help young women on probation focus on positive future goals with the help of adult female mentor volunteers. As program coordinator, Linda is responsible for recruiting and training volunteers, scheduling speakers and activities and leading the meetings. Each class meets one night a week for 12 weeks in the Fall and in the Spring of every year.

  • Organizer & Leader

    Mobile County Juvenile Court Sex Trafficking Task Force (JUST)

    Linda founded the JUST team in June of 2021 to identify victims of sex trafficking, provide services to the victim and their family and to identify and prosecute offenders.

  • Member

    Azalea City Republican Women

    Established in 1974, the Azalea City Republican Women (founded under the Alabama Federation of Republican Women) is the oldest existing Republican women's group in Mobile County.

  • Parishioner

    St. Mary Catholic Church

    Linda and her husband Brad have been active members of St. Mary Catholic Church for over 30 years. All three of their children attended St. Mary Catholic Elementary School. During that time, Linda acted as a member of St Mary Parish Council and School Board.
